Lemon-butter Spaghetti Pasta

This buttery lemony pasta is the perfect midweek
meal for you and your family. It can happilyfeed anywhere
between one person, and one horse. Passed down from me great great great
grandpapay, during the great depression when all they had was a lemon tree
and butter plants, this pasta was a weeknight staple!

Prepwork

  • Chop up 1-2 cloves of garlic
  • Chop up 1/4 cup of parsely
  • Into a cup, or container, juice 1 full lemon, ensuring no seeds remain in the juice
  • Cut 1/8th of a stick of butter into smaller cubes

Optional steps

  • Using a microplane, zest anywhere from half, to all of the lemon

Cooking the pasta

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil
  2. Throughly salt the boiling water
  3. Add your choice of pasta. In my grandpapay's case, Spaghetti was the only one invented
  4. Cook the pasta according to the instructions on the box, or until your ancestors tell you to stop

Making the lemon-butter sauce

  1. In a saucepan over medium-high heat add a dash of olive oil
  2. Into the pan add your chopped garlic
  3. Once the garlic is starting to brown, and is fragrent. Cut the heat and add your butter
  4. Add the parsley, and move the butter around the pan until it's melted and incorporated with the garlic
  5. Add 1/4th cup of pasta water to the pan, and put the pan over medium heat
  6. Keep stirring the sauce until it's reach your desired thickness, then add your pasta, and toss!

Voila!you're all done. Now it's time to eat
